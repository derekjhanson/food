Moroccan Chicken Tagine

The Hansons

An instant pot version of the classic Morrocan cuisine.

Traditionally cooked in a clay pot (pictures). Outside of Morocco many use a Dutch Oven, but this adaptation uses an instant pot.

History of Tagine

 

The Steps

  • Season the chicken (~ 1.5 lbs)
  • Saute to sear the spices to sides of chicken and get darkened appearance on the meat
  • Saute garlic, ginger, carrots, and onions (by selves - so take out chicken first)
  • Turn off saute, then add ingredients: chicken, water/broth, quinoa, chickpeas, tomatoes, peas, drizzle of honey, and your choice of fruit (cherries, dates, raisins, apricot - depending on taste)
  • Set pressure cook 10 minutes (if 1.5 lbs of chicken)

Spices

  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

- should actually use Harissa (mixture of spices) - see additional post for instructions on how to make or where to buy

Other ingredients

  • 1.5 lbs chicken (breasts, thighs, your choice)
  • 1.5 cups chicken broth (or water)
  • 2 garlic cloves minced
  • 1/2 cup finely diced onions
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • drizzle of honey to help saute the veggies
  • 2 Roma tomatoes, diced
  • 1/2 cup peas (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon ginger minced
  • 1/3 cup uncooked quinoa
  • 1 can chickpeas drained
  • 1/2 cup dried fruit of choice
  • optional garnish: finely chopped cilantro leaves
