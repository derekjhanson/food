Peach Wine

by The Hansons
published on January 5, 2020

Yummmmmmy peach wine (https://practicalselfreliance.com/peach-wine/)

AmountIngredient
1 gallon (10+?)very ripe peaches
3 lbssugar
12raisins (yeast nutrient)
1/2 cupblack tea (tannin)
1/4 packetchampagne yeast
  

Rinse and cut up peaches (slice into thin apple-chip like slices for best extraction?).

Add sugar, stir every few minutes for 2 hours + (until maximum syrup/juice extracted)

Pour liquid into fermenters around 2/3 full each. Then, add water to peaches (to get some of that extra sugar out) and fill jars to 3/4 full.

Next, add warmed yeast. Then add lemon juice (equivalent for each jar), raisins (3 per quart jar), and tea (enough to slightly darken each jar).

Seal up with carboy/airlock for 10-14 days in primary fermentation.

Siphon and transfer to secondary where fermentation should take 6 weeks minimum (3-6 months suggested)

Siphon to bottle and age as desired.

 

Made:

1/5/20 - chopped 6 peaches of different levels of ripeness (though generally ripe). Used unmeasured amount of sugar and moved to primary fermenter (quart jar) after 8 hours of occasional stirring. Sugary mixture filled 2/3 of jar (perhaps too much, should water down to fermet faster next time?). Followed lemon juice and raisins (might be too few of these), as suggested. Raisins added before yeast (some suggest otherwise).
